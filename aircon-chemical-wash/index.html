What it involves

What happens during a chemical wash

A chemical wash is a full teardown and deep clean of your aircon's indoor unit. Unlike a standard service that cleans the surface, a chemical wash reaches the parts that routine maintenance can't — the evaporator coil, internal drainage channels, and blower components that accumulate mould and bacteria over time.

Our technician fully dismantles the indoor unit, flushes the evaporator coil with chemical solution to break down buildup, high-pressure washes every component, treats for mould and bacteria, flushes the entire drainage system, and runs a gas level and performance test before reassembling.

You get before-and-after photos of every unit. The whole job takes 45–60 minutes per unit. When we're done, your aircon should cool faster, smell clean, and run quieter.

Do you need one?

Signs you need a chemical wash

A standard service won't fix these — you need a chemical wash.

Aircon smells musty or mouldy

Mould and bacteria have built up on the coil and in the drainage system. Routine cleaning won't reach it. A chemical wash with mould treatment clears it fully.

Water leaking from the unit

A blocked or partially clogged drainage system causes water to overflow from the tray. A full drainage flush during chemical wash clears the blockage completely.

Not cooling as well as before

A heavily fouled evaporator coil reduces airflow and heat exchange. If your room takes much longer to cool down, a chemical flush often restores full cooling capacity.

Not serviced in over a year

If your aircon hasn't been properly serviced in 12+ months, mould and buildup will have accumulated beyond what a standard service can address. Start with a chemical wash to reset it.

Recommended frequency

How often should you do a chemical wash?

In Singapore's climate, a chemical wash every 6–12 months is recommended for most households — in addition to a standard service every 3 months. The heat and humidity accelerate mould, bacteria, and mineral buildup inside your aircon, especially on the evaporator coil and in the drainage channels.

If you run your aircon more than 8 hours a day, have pets, or notice any smell or reduced cooling, lean toward every 6 months. For lightly used units in air-conditioned offices or bedrooms, once a year is usually sufficient.

After the first chemical wash, maintaining with regular standard services every 3 months will slow down future buildup — meaning your next chemical wash won't need to be as intensive.

How much does an aircon chemical wash cost in Singapore?+
AFIX charges $60 for a single unit and $50/unit for 2 or more units. This includes full dismantling, chemical flush, high-pressure wash, mould treatment, drainage flush, gas test, before-and-after photos, and shoe covers. No hidden charges.
What is an aircon chemical wash?+
A chemical wash is a deep-cleaning process where the indoor unit is fully dismantled, the evaporator coil is flushed with chemical solution, all components are high-pressure washed, and mould and bacteria are treated. It reaches areas that standard servicing cannot, and is recommended when the aircon smells, leaks, or isn't cooling effectively.
How long does a chemical wash take?+
Approximately 45–60 minutes per unit. The indoor unit needs to be fully dismantled, cleaned, and carefully reassembled — this takes longer than a standard 20–30 minute service.
When do I need a chemical wash instead of a normal service?+
Choose a chemical wash if your aircon smells musty or mouldy, is leaking water, is not cooling as well as before, or hasn't been properly serviced in over a year. A standard service only cleans the surface — a chemical wash removes deep-seated mould, bacteria, and buildup that routine cleaning cannot reach.
How often should I do a chemical wash in Singapore?+
Every 6–12 months, in addition to a standard service every 3 months. Singapore's humidity accelerates mould growth inside aircon units. Heavy users (8+ hours/day or pets) should do it every 6 months; lighter users can go once a year.
Will a chemical wash fix my aircon if it's not cold?+
A chemical wash restores cooling if the cause is a dirty evaporator coil blocking airflow. If the issue is low refrigerant gas or a faulty compressor, those need to be addressed separately. We include a gas level and performance test with every chemical wash and will advise you clearly on any findings — no obligation to proceed with additional work.
