What we do

What's included in every aircon service

Every aircon service — regardless of tier — includes shoe covers and floor protection before we touch anything. We take before-and-after photos so you can see exactly what was done. No extra charge for this.

For a standard service: we wash and disinfect the air filters, clean the evaporator coil, clear the drainage tray and piping, clean the blower wheel, vacuum the drainage line, and run a compressor pressure check. The whole job takes 20–30 minutes per unit.

For a chemical wash: the indoor unit is fully dismantled. The evaporator coil is flushed with chemical solution, all components are high-pressure washed, mould and bacteria are treated, the full drainage system is flushed, and a gas level and performance test is run. Allow 45–60 minutes per unit.

If we spot anything that needs attention — low gas, a cracked drain tray, unusual compressor readings — we'll flag it clearly and quote separately. We won't do extra work without your approval.

Maintenance guide

How often should you service your aircon in Singapore?

Singapore's heat and humidity mean aircon units work harder than in most countries — and collect dust, mould, and bacteria faster. Most manufacturers and NEA recommend servicing every 3 months for residential units.

If you run your aircon for more than 8 hours a day, or if there are pets in the household, every 2 months is better. Neglecting servicing forces the compressor to work harder, raises your electricity bill, and shortens the unit's lifespan.

A chemical wash every 6–12 months deals with buildup that routine cleaning can't reach — especially important if the unit is more than 2 years old or showing signs of reduced cooling or smell.

Troubleshooting

Common aircon problems we fix

Servicing fixes most common issues. Here's what the symptom usually means.

Aircon not cold

Usually caused by a dirty evaporator coil blocking airflow, or low refrigerant gas. A chemical wash restores coil performance. If gas is low, we quote a top-up separately after diagnosing the cause — low gas often means a leak that needs finding first.

Water leaking from aircon

Almost always a blocked drainage line or overflowing drain tray. We clear this as part of every standard service. If the drain tray is cracked or the pipe is disconnected, we'll show you and quote a fix before touching anything.

Aircon smells bad

Musty or foul smells mean mould and bacteria have built up on the coil and in the drainage system — routine cleaning won't reach it. A chemical wash with mould treatment clears it fully. Most units are odour-free within 30 minutes of the job finishing.

Aircon making noise

Rattling is usually a loose panel or debris in the blower. A humming or grinding sound points to the fan motor or compressor. We inspect and diagnose on-site. Panel and blower issues are fixed during the service visit — motor or compressor work is quoted separately.

How often should I service my aircon in Singapore?+
Every 3 months is the standard recommendation for Singapore's climate. If you run your aircon heavily (8+ hours/day) or have pets, every 2 months is better. An annual chemical wash every 6–12 months is also recommended to deal with deeper buildup.
What is the difference between standard servicing and a chemical wash?+
Standard servicing covers filter cleaning, coil cleaning, drainage clearing, and a basic performance check — good for routine upkeep. A chemical wash goes much further: the unit is fully dismantled, the evaporator coil is flushed with chemical solution, all components are high-pressure washed, and mould and bacteria are treated. Choose a chemical wash if your aircon smells, leaks, or isn't cooling properly.

How long does an aircon service take?+
A standard service takes about 20–30 minutes per unit. A chemical wash takes 45–60 minutes per unit since the unit needs to be fully dismantled, cleaned with chemical solution, and carefully reassembled.
Why is my aircon still not cold after servicing?+
If cooling doesn't improve after a standard service, the likely cause is low refrigerant gas or a faulty compressor — neither is resolved by cleaning alone.
